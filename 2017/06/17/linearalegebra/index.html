1. Vectors, what even are they?

向量究竟是什么？

In computer science perspective is that vectors are ordered listed of numbers. For example, let’s say you were doing some analytics about house prices.

$$Vectors \quad \Leftrightarrow \quad Lists\quad of \quad number$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \quad
\begin{bmatrix}5 \\ 0 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}
$$

And the only features you cared about were square footage and price. You might modle each house with a pair of numbers: the first indicating square footage, and the second indicating price. Notice the order matters here. In the lingo, you’d be houses as two-dimensional vectors. Where in this context, “vector” is pretty much just a fancy word for “list” and what makes it two-dimensional is the fact that the length of that list is 2.

$$
\begin{bmatrix}2.600 ft^2 \\ $300,000 \end{bmatrix} \quad \neq \quad \begin{bmatrix}$300,000 \\ 2.600 ft^2 \end{bmatrix}
$$

Linear Alegebra gives the data analyst a nice way to conceptualize many lists of numbers in a visual way which can seriously clarify pattern in data, and give a global view of what certain opeartions do. And on the flip side, it gives people like phsicsists and computer graphics programmers a language. To describle a space and manipulation of space using numbers that can be crunched and run through a computer.

2. Linear combinations, span and bases

向量的线性组合、张成的空间与基

$i$ and $j$ are the “basis vectors” of the $xy$ coordinate system

  • “Linear combination” of $\vec v$ and $\vec w$

a$\vec v + b\vec w$ (a and b are scalars)

  • The “span” of $\vec v$ and $\vec w$ is the set of all their linear combinations.

a$\vec v + b\vec w$ (let a and b vary over all real numbers)

  • Linearly independent
    $\vec w \neq a\vec v$ (For all values of a)

  • Technical defination of basis(基): The basis of a vector space is a set of linear independent vectors that span the full space.

3. Matrices as linear transformations

线性变换与矩阵

Unfortunately, no one can be told what the matrix is. You have to see it for yourself. –Morpheus
(Suprisingly, apt words on the importance of understanding matrix operations visually)

Linear trasformation

To start, just parse this term, transformation is essentially a fancy word for “function”. It’s something that takes input and spites out an output for each one.
The word that “transformation” suggests that you think using the word “movement”.
Linear: 1. all lines must remains lines, without getting curved. 2. origin remains fixed.

A two dimensioal linear transformation is completely described by just four numbers:
the two coordinates for where i-hat lands and two coordinates for where j-hat lands.

$$\vec i=
\begin{bmatrix} 3\\ -2 \end{bmatrix} \quad
\vec j =
\begin{bmatrix} 2\\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \quad
$$

It’s common to package these coordinates into a 2-by-2 grid of numbers, called a 2-by-2 Matrix.

$$
Matrix =
\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}
$$

And you want to know where that linear transformation takes that vector.

$$
Vector =
\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}
$$

You can take the coordinates of the vector. Multipy them by the corresponding columns of the matrix, then add together what you get.

$$
\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} *
\begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} =
5 \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} +
7 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}
$$

This corresponds with the idea of adding the scaled version of our new basis vectors.
